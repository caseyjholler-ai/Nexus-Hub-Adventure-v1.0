Tacit Knowledge Extraction Protocol

A Structured System for Converting Expert Knowledge into Trainable AI Learning Units

Designed by Casey Holler | December 2025

Overview

The Tacit Knowledge Extraction Protocol (TKEP) is a structured methodology for extracting implicit expert knowledge into trainable, bankable learning units. Designed to address the expert bottleneck in ML training data generation, TKEP enables efficient knowledge transfer through time-boxed sessions with human-in-the-loop validation.

Problem Statement

Expert knowledge is time-constrained and often implicit:

  • Experts struggle to articulate intuitions that have become automatic
  • Traditional documentation assumes shared context that novices lack
  • Q&A sessions are inefficient (novice doesn't know what to ask)
  • Expert time is expensive and scarce at scale

The bottleneck: How to extract maximum value from minimal expert time while maintaining accuracy and usability of captured knowledge.

The Five-Phase Extraction Cycle

Phase 1: FRAME (1-2 minutes)

Purpose: Establish shared context and identify vocabulary gaps

Process:

  • Expert names one concept to explain (in their own terminology)
  • Learner/interviewer states what they're trying to understand
  • Protocol identifies gap between framings
  • Gap becomes the extraction focus

Output: Topic captured in both vocabularies, with bridge points identified

Phase 2: EXTRACT (3-5 minutes)

Purpose: Surface tacit knowledge through structured questioning

Extraction Question Categories:

  • Process Questions: "What's the FIRST thing you check?"
  • Decision Questions: "How do you choose between approach A vs. B?"
  • Translation Questions: "What analogy would you use for a 12-year-old?"
  • Edge Case Questions: "When does the normal approach NOT work?"
  • Meta Questions: "What do you wish someone had told you?"

Output: Raw expert knowledge with explicit tacit components identified

Phase 3: TRANSLATE (2-3 minutes)

Purpose: Human-in-the-loop validation through paraphrasing

Process:

  • Learner/interviewer paraphrases expert's explanation in own words
  • Expert confirms accuracy or corrects misunderstanding
  • Cycle repeats until mutual understanding achieved
  • Mismatches reveal hidden assumptions or unclear language

Critical Function: This is the validation layer preventing knowledge corruption

Phase 4: CHUNK (1-2 minutes)

Purpose: Package knowledge into discrete, reusable learning units

Standardized Chunk Format:

  • Chunk ID (for tracking/linking)
  • Core Concept (one clear sentence)
  • Concrete Example (real-world illustration)
  • Prerequisites (what learner needs first)
  • Common Misconception (preventive wisdom)
  • Practice Prompt (verification test)
  • Time to Learn (realistic estimate)
  • Connections (links to related chunks)

Phase 5: BANK (30 seconds)

Purpose: Store chunks in searchable, refinable repository

Process:

  • Save chunk with metadata (tags, timestamps, participant IDs)
  • Link to related chunks (builds knowledge graph)
  • Track access patterns (enables spaced repetition)

Output: Searchable knowledge bank that compounds over time

Key Design Principles

TIME-BOXING (5-10 Minutes Per Session)

Cognitive load constraint maximizes signal, minimizes expert fatigue

BIDIRECTIONAL LEARNING

Expert learns what's unclear. Learner learns new concepts. Both improve through feedback loop.

ANTI-REWARD-HACKING PATTERNS

Human-in-the-loop validation prevents knowledge corruption. No shortcuts possible.

PROGRESSIVE DECOMPOSITION

Complex concepts become digestible through deliberate chunking

LIVING DOCUMENTATION

Knowledge bank is searchable, refinable, and reusable
