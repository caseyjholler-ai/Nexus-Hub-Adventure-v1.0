Tacit Knowledge Extraction Protocol

A Structured System for Converting Expert Knowledge into Trainable AI Learning Units

Designed by Casey Holler | December 2024

Overview

The Tacit Knowledge Extraction Protocol (TKEP) is a structured methodology for extracting implicit expert knowledge into trainable, bankable learning units. Designed to address the expert bottleneck in ML training data generation, TKEP enables efficient knowledge transfer through time-boxed sessions with human-in-the-loop validation.

Key Design Principles

TIME-BOXING

Cognitive load constraint maximizes signal, minimizes expert fatigue

BIDIRECTIONAL LEARNING

Expert learns what's unclear. Learner learns concepts. Both improve through feedback.

ANTI-REWARD-HACKING

Human-in-the-loop validation prevents knowledge corruption

PROGRESSIVE DECOMPOSITION

Complex concepts become digestible through deliberate chunking
